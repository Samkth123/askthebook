Editorial Policy

How we maintain accuracy, neutrality, and scholarly standards in our content.

Content Types and Standards

Ask the Holy Book publishes two types of content:

Structured Articles

Our articles section contains human-written, editorially reviewed content. These articles follow academic writing standards: clear structure, multiple perspectives, historical grounding, proper citations, and neutral tone. Articles are typically 900-1500 words and cover topics such as hermeneutics, historical context, comparative religion, and ethical frameworks.

Editorial Process: Articles undergo review for accuracy, neutrality, and clarity before publication. We verify citations, check historical claims against scholarly sources, and ensure multiple perspectives are represented where relevant.

AI-Generated Q&A Responses

Our AI system generates answers to user questions using retrieval-augmented generation (RAG). These responses are clearly marked as AI-generated and include citations to source texts.

Quality Standards: AI responses must include at least three citations, provide historical context where relevant, and maintain neutral tone. Responses under 500 words or lacking proper citations are not indexed for search engines.

Human Review: While AI responses are generated automatically, we monitor for accuracy and quality. Users can report errors through our contact form, and we review flagged content.

How AI Is Used

Our AI system serves as a research assistant, not a content creator. Here's how it works:

Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG)

The AI uses semantic search to find relevant passages from the Bible, Quran, or Torah based on the user's question. It then generates a response that:

  • Cites specific passages with chapter-and-verse references
  • Provides context about when and why passages were written
  • Presents multiple interpretations when they exist
  • Maintains neutral, academic tone

AI Limitations

We are transparent about AI limitations:

  • AI may produce errors or incomplete answers
  • AI cannot replace human scholarly judgment
  • AI responses should be verified against source texts
  • AI may not represent all interpretive traditions

We encourage users to read cited passages in full and consult scholarly sources for deeper study.

Source Methodology

Scripture Sources

We use public domain translations of the Bible, Quran, and Torah. For the Bible, we primarily use the King James Version and American Standard Version. For the Quran, we use established English translations. For the Torah, we use the same sources as the Bible (first five books).

All passages are cited with standard reference formats: Book Chapter:Verse for Bible/Torah, and Surah:Ayah for Quran.

Historical and Scholarly Sources

When providing historical context or scholarly perspectives, we draw from:

  • Academic religious studies publications
  • Historical scholarship on ancient Near Eastern contexts
  • Comparative religion research
  • Established reference works (e.g., academic encyclopedias)

We avoid relying solely on devotional sources or single-denominational perspectives.

Fact-Checking and Accuracy

Citation Verification

All citations are verified against source texts. We check that:

  • References exist and are correctly formatted
  • Quoted passages match source texts
  • Context is accurately represented

Historical Claims

Historical information is checked against scholarly sources. When historical scholarship presents multiple viewpoints, we acknowledge this rather than presenting one view as definitive.

Error Correction Process

When errors are identified:

  1. We review the reported error
  2. Verify the correction against source texts or scholarly sources
  3. Update the content promptly
  4. Note corrections when significant

Neutrality and Multiple Perspectives

We maintain neutrality by:

  • Avoiding devotional language: We do not use prayerful or worshipful language.
  • Presenting multiple interpretations: When interpretations vary, we present different viewpoints.
  • Respecting all traditions: We treat Judaism, Christianity, and Islam with equal respect.
  • Acknowledging diversity: We recognize that interpretations vary within and across traditions.
  • Avoiding advocacy: We do not advocate for particular theological positions.

User Feedback Process

We welcome feedback on:

  • Factual errors
  • Missing perspectives
  • Clarity and readability
  • Suggestions for new articles or topics

Users can submit feedback through our contact page. We review all submissions and respond to substantive feedback.

Content Updates

We regularly update content to:

  • Correct errors
  • Add new scholarly perspectives
  • Expand articles with additional information
  • Improve clarity and readability

Major updates are noted, and users can see when articles were last updated.

Transparency

We are transparent about:

  • Which content is AI-generated versus human-written
  • Our source methodology
  • Our limitations and disclaimers
  • How we handle errors

Editorial Independence

Ask the Holy Book is editorially independent. We are not affiliated with any religious institution, denomination, or advocacy organization. Our content decisions are based on scholarly standards and user needs, not external influence.

Contact

For questions about our editorial policy or to report concerns, please visit our contact page.
