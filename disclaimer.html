Important information about the purposes, limitations, and appropriate use of Ask the Holy Book.

Informational Purposes Only

Ask the Holy Book provides informational content about the Bible, Quran, and Torah for educational and research purposes. The content on this platform is intended to help users understand these texts, their historical contexts, and various interpretations.

This platform is not intended to serve as:

  • Religious instruction or guidance
  • Spiritual counseling
  • Legal advice
  • Medical advice
  • Professional theological consultation

Not Religious Counseling

Ask the Holy Book does not provide religious counseling, spiritual guidance, or pastoral care. For matters of personal faith, religious practice, spiritual questions, or life decisions informed by religious principles, users should consult:

  • Qualified clergy or religious leaders
  • Religious scholars or theologians
  • Spiritual counselors or advisors
  • Religious community leaders

Our platform provides information; it does not replace the guidance of trained religious professionals who can address personal circumstances, provide pastoral care, and offer context-specific advice.

Not Legal Advice

While we may discuss ethical principles, moral frameworks, or legal concepts mentioned in sacred texts, this information does not constitute legal advice. For legal matters, users should consult:

  • Licensed attorneys
  • Legal professionals
  • Relevant legal authorities

Historical or scriptural discussions of law do not constitute advice on contemporary legal matters.

Not Medical Advice

Any discussion of health, healing, or medical practices in historical or scriptural contexts is for informational purposes only. For medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment, users must consult:

  • Licensed healthcare professionals
  • Medical doctors
  • Qualified health practitioners

Do not use information from this platform to make medical decisions or replace professional medical care.

Interpretations Vary by Tradition

Religious texts are interpreted differently across:

  • Different religious traditions
  • Denominations within traditions
  • Scholarly schools of thought
  • Historical periods
  • Cultural contexts

We present multiple perspectives but cannot represent every interpretive tradition. Users should be aware that:

  • Interpretations vary significantly
  • Different communities may understand passages differently
  • Scholarly consensus evolves
  • Personal or community interpretation may differ from academic perspectives

We encourage users to consult multiple sources and engage with their own religious communities for guidance on interpretation.

AI-Generated Content Limitations

Portions of our content are generated using artificial intelligence. While we use advanced systems and quality controls, AI-generated content has limitations:

  • May contain errors: AI systems can produce factual errors or incomplete information.
  • Requires verification: All AI-generated content should be verified against source texts.
  • Not comprehensive: AI responses may not represent all relevant perspectives or interpretations.
  • Context limitations: AI may not fully capture historical or cultural context.
  • No human judgment: AI cannot exercise the nuanced judgment of human scholars.

We clearly mark AI-generated content and encourage users to verify information independently.
