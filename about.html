About Ask the Holy Book

A scholarly platform for exploring the Bible, Quran, and Torah through structured articles, comparative analysis, and AI-assisted inquiry.

Mission and Purpose

Ask the Holy Book serves as an educational resource for understanding the foundational texts of Judaism, Christianity, and Islam. Our platform combines structured scholarly articles with AI-assisted question answering to provide accurate, cited information about these sacred texts.

We aim to present information neutrally, respecting all traditions while maintaining academic rigor. The platform is designed for students, researchers, interfaith dialogue participants, and anyone seeking to understand how these texts address ethical, theological, and practical questions.

What We Provide

Structured Articles

Our articles section contains in-depth explorations of topics such as hermeneutics, historical context, comparative religion, and ethical frameworks. Each article follows academic standards: clear structure, multiple perspectives, historical grounding, and proper citations.

AI-Assisted Q&A

Our AI system uses retrieval-augmented generation (RAG) to find relevant passages from the source texts and provide answers with citations. The AI is a tool for exploration, not a replacement for scholarly study or religious guidance.

Comparative Analysis

We facilitate side-by-side comparison of how the Bible, Quran, and Torah address similar themes, enabling users to understand both shared principles and distinct perspectives across traditions.

Methodology

Our approach combines several methods:

  • Text-first retrieval: Answers begin with relevant passages from the source texts, identified through semantic search.
  • Citation requirement: Every answer includes specific chapter-and-verse references (or surah-and-ayah for the Quran).
  • Contextual framing: We provide historical and cultural context where relevant.
  • Multiple perspectives: When interpretations vary, we present different scholarly viewpoints.
  • Neutral presentation: We avoid devotional language and maintain academic tone.

Who Runs This Site

Ask the Holy Book is operated by a team committed to religious education and interfaith understanding. Our contributors include individuals with backgrounds in religious studies, theology, and comparative religion. We are not affiliated with any particular religious institution or denomination.

Our goal is to serve as a bridge between traditions, providing accurate information that respects the diversity of interpretation within and across faith communities.

Background and Goals

The platform emerged from a recognition that reliable, neutral information about sacred texts can be difficult to find online. Many resources are either devotional (presenting one tradition's perspective) or overly simplified (lacking depth and citations).

We aim to fill this gap by providing:

  • Accurate citations to source texts
  • Historical and cultural context
  • Recognition of interpretive diversity
  • Comparative perspectives across traditions
  • Accessible but scholarly language

Limitations and Disclaimers

Not Religious Counseling: This platform provides informational content. It is not a substitute for consultation with clergy, religious scholars, or counselors. For matters of personal faith, religious practice, or spiritual guidance, please consult qualified religious leaders.

Not Legal or Medical Advice: While we may discuss ethical principles from sacred texts, this information does not constitute legal or medical advice. Consult appropriate professionals for such matters.

Interpretive Diversity: Religious texts are interpreted differently across communities, denominations, and scholarly traditions. We present multiple perspectives but cannot represent every viewpoint. Users should be aware that interpretations vary.

AI Limitations: Our AI system, while sophisticated, may produce errors or incomplete answers. All AI-generated content should be verified against source texts. We encourage users to read cited passages in full context.

Historical Context: We provide historical background, but historical scholarship itself evolves. Our information reflects current academic understanding, which may be refined as research progresses.

Content Standards

We maintain several content standards:

  • Accuracy: We strive for factual accuracy in citations and historical information.
  • Neutrality: We present information without advocating for particular theological positions.
  • Respect: We treat all traditions with respect, avoiding dismissive or derogatory language.
  • Transparency: We clearly indicate when content is AI-generated versus human-written articles.
  • Correction: We welcome corrections and update content when errors are identified.

How to Use This Platform

Users can engage with Ask the Holy Book in several ways:

  1. Read Articles: Browse our structured articles on topics like hermeneutics, ethics, and comparative religion.
  2. Ask Questions: Use the AI Q&A feature to explore specific questions, always verifying citations against source texts.
  3. Compare Traditions: Use the comparison feature to see how different texts address similar themes.
  4. Explore Topics: Navigate topic pages to find curated content on specific themes.

Feedback and Corrections

We welcome feedback, corrections, and suggestions. If you identify an error in citation, historical information, or interpretation, please contact us through our contact page. We review all submissions and update content accordingly.

Future Development

We are continuously expanding our article library, refining our AI system, and improving the platform's usability. Planned additions include more articles on hermeneutics, expanded historical context sections, and enhanced comparative analysis tools.
