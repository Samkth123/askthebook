What is Three-Way Scripture Comparison?

Comparing Bible, Quran, and Torah together provides a comprehensive view of how the three major Abrahamic traditions address the same question. This three-way comparison reveals shared principles, distinct perspectives, and the rich diversity of interpretation across these foundational texts.

When you compare Bible, Quran, and Torah, you receive side-by-side answers showing how each tradition approaches your question. Each answer includes specific citations: chapter-and-verse from the Bible and Torah, and surah-and-verse from the Quran.

This comparison tool is perfect for interfaith dialogue, academic study, comparative religion courses, and personal exploration. It helps you understand both the common ground and the unique contributions of each tradition.

How to Compare Bible, Quran, and Torah

  1. Type your question in the search box
  2. Select all three sources: "Bible," "Quran," and "Torah"
  3. Click "Ask" to receive three-column answers
  4. Review citations from each text

The three-way comparison shows you exactly how each tradition addresses your question, making it easy to see similarities and differences across all three texts. Each answer includes specific citations so you can verify the passages yourself.

What You Get When You Compare All Three
